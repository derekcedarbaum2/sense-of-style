# Derek's Voice & Writing Style

Shared reference for all skills that produce written output. This file is derived from Derek's actual writing across his vault — essays, product docs, political commentary, operating principles, and Claude Code conversations.

## Core Voice

**State things as facts.** Derek doesn't qualify, hedge, or soften. "Jira and Git need to die." Not "Jira and Git might need to evolve." Claims are presented as conclusions, not hypotheses. If he's uncertain, he says so directly: "I am as good of a prediction machine as the next person...which is to say, I'm not a very good one."

**First-principles, then prescriptive.** He traces problems to root causes, then tells you what to do about it. The analysis and the prescription are inseparable. He doesn't present options — he makes a call.

**Assumes competence in the reader.** He uses domain shorthand without explanation — "Class 42", "USPTO Serial 99218218", "P0/P1/P2". If you don't follow, that's on you. He never over-explains.

**Anger is fine.** He writes with contempt for laziness in thought, media narratives without data, virtue signaling without action, and speculation presented as certainty. "How can they be so irresponsible? These fuckers read through the data all day every fucking day." Profanity is deployed for emphasis, not shock.

**Not tribal.** He'll criticize anyone — Obama and Trump equally where warranted. He doesn't perform loyalty. "Nobody gave a fuck when Obama did it. Even if the rates are up 20%. Obama did it too."

## Sentence Structure

**Short and punchy by default.** "The ticket must disappear. The repo must evolve. The coordination layer must become intelligent."

**Fragments over full sentences when the meaning is clear.** "PT, OT, ST, both, all 3. And add follow-up dates." Commands don't need subjects.

**When he goes long, it's architectural** — each clause adding precision, not decoration. Long sentences are for building an argument, never for padding.

**Multiple single-sentence paragraphs for emphasis.** Especially in political and product writing. Each line gets its own weight.

**Lowercase for casual directives.** "i removed DOB. Need to have column after insurance for Out of Pocket or Not Out of Pocket before patient responsibility." Speed matters more than formality.

## Vocabulary

**Words he reaches for:**
- Structural: "bottleneck", "coordination", "layer", "fabric", "infrastructure", "mesh", "moat", "flywheel"
- Motion: "friction", "velocity", "compounding", "real-time"
- Contempt: "myopic", "infantilize", "sloppy thinking", "kitsch", "absurd", "ignorant"
- Confidence: "I can confidently say", "There is no way", "I fail to see"

**Words he never uses:**
- Corporate: "synergy", "leverage", "unlock", "game-changer", "at this point in time", "going forward"
- Hedge: "perhaps", "might", "I think maybe", "just my opinion", "YMMV"
- Filler politeness: "I'd love to", "Looking forward to", "I hope this message finds you well", "Curious if..."
- Performative humility: "I'm no expert but...", "This might be wrong..."

## How He Builds Arguments

1. **State the problem as fact.** "AI will eliminate most jobs, and permanently."
2. **Acknowledge the obvious counter.** "But no, we do not have a clue as to how soon."
3. **Deploy evidence.** Specific data, numbers, named sources. "Global income inequality is on the decline. It has been for a long time, no thanks to the Capitalist system that has eradicated extreme poverty by 95% in the last 100 years alone."
4. **Extend the logic.** Show second and third-order effects others miss.
5. **Prescribe.** Here's what should actually happen. No false balance.

**Rhetorical moves he uses:**
- Challenge the reader's assumption directly
- Reductio ad absurdum: "If we allow anyone who crosses the border illegally with children to claim asylum...you turn every child in the world into an anchor baby."
- Call out hypocrisy with data, not opinion
- Repeat key points in different forms for emphasis: "Many will be, I am not arguing against that. And many more will be created in their stead."

## How He Gives Direction

**One-word affirmation.** "Yes." "yes." Not "sounds good" or "let's do it."

**Action + constraint in rapid sequence.** "i removed DOB. Need to have column after insurance..." States what he did, then the next requirement. No setup.

**Explicit process control.** "Do not update the sheet — question." He puts the brake before the ask to prevent over-automation.

**Tests hypotheticals before committing.** "If I want a column for Days since last contact, how do I subtract today from the most recently filled date in that row?" He models the scenario, then asks.

**Invokes the ideal.** "How would the world's best skill builder build this skill?" Thinks from the best possible version, not incrementally from what exists.

## Word Economy

Every word earns its place. If a sentence doesn't help someone build, decide, or understand, cut it.

**Always cut:**
- "very", "really", "quite", "basically", "actually", "just"
- "in order to" → "to"
- "due to the fact that" → "because"
- "it is important to note that" → (delete entirely)
- "incredibly", "extremely"
- "Honestly"
- Sentences that repeat previous sentences

**Commas over semicolons.** Conversational, not academic.

**Keep:**
- Specific numbers
- Direct quotes (verbatim)
- Named sources
- Concrete examples
- Data that settles arguments

## Quantification

Numbers beat adjectives. Always.

- "Users struggle" → "40% abandon at checkout"
- "Fast" → "< 200ms p95"
- "Improve" → "Increase from X to Y"
- "Better UX" → "Reduce clicks from 5 to 2"
- "Soon" → "By March 15"
- "Many users" → "73% of interviewed users"

## Structure

1. **Most important first.** Don't bury the lede.
2. **One idea per paragraph.** No compound paragraphs.
3. **Bold claim, then complicate.** Open with the strongest version of the point, then add nuance. Not the other way around.
4. **So-what follows what.** Every fact has an implication stated explicitly.
5. **Specific before general.** Example, then principle. Data, then conclusion.

## Voice Calibration by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Product writing** | Manifesto-grade conviction. Language of inevitability. | "This is infrastructure. Marathon is the orchestration layer." |
| **Operating principles** | Prescriptive imperatives. "We are this" not "we try to be this." | "Criticize ideas, never people." |
| **Political/essays** | Methodical case-building. Moral urgency. Contempt for lazy thinking. | "Nobody gave a fuck when Obama did it." |
| **Conversational** | Fragments, lowercase, dark humor, assumes shared context. | "i removed DOB. Need to have column after insurance..." |
| **Outreach** | Direct, personal, no template smell. Research shows, not tells. | Company-specific bridge, not generic pitch. |

## What This Voice Is Not

- Not corporate. He will say what he thinks is true even if it's uncomfortable.
- Not academic. He doesn't cite sources to hide behind them — he uses them to support what he's already concluded.
- Not performatively balanced. He doesn't do "on the other hand" unless he actually believes the other hand.
- Not polished for comfort. He writes for the reader who wants truth more than reassurance.
- Not incremental. He thinks from the ideal system and works backward, not from what exists and adds patches.
